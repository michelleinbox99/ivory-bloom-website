PART 1

Get on Google Maps

This is your Google Business Profile — the free listing that shows your pin on Maps and your card in local search results. It's the single highest-leverage thing on this page.

~60–90 minutes to set up Free Needs a Google account

  1. Start your profile at business.google.com

    Sign in with the Google account you want tied to the business (create a dedicated one if you don't already have a business Gmail). Search for "Ivory & Bloom Atelier" first — if a profile already exists (sometimes Google auto-creates one from directory data), you'll claim it instead of creating a duplicate.

  2. Choose "service-area business," not storefront

    Since Gainesville is where you work from but not a public shop, tell Google you serve customers at their location / by shipping, rather than listing a walk-in storefront. You'll set Gainesville, VA as your base and can list the wider area you're willing to serve (e.g. Northern Virginia, or nationwide since you ship).

  3. Pick your category carefully

    Primary category: Bridal Shop. Add secondary categories that match what you actually sell — Gift Shop, Craft Store. Category is one of the strongest signals for which searches you show up in, more than most of the text on your listing.

  4. Verify the profile

    Google will offer verification by phone, email, video, or postcard depending on your business type. Postcard (mailed to your address) is common for service-area businesses and takes 5–14 days — start this step early since nothing else goes live until you're verified.

  5. Fill in every field, not just the required ones

    Business description (use it to naturally include "handmade," "Gainesville, Virginia," "bridal pouches," "flower crowns"), website URL, phone, hours you're reachable, and at least 10 photos — your product shots and the real wedding photography you already have work well here. Incomplete profiles rank worse.

  6. Ask happy customers to leave a Google review

    Separate from your Etsy or site reviews — Google reviews specifically feed your Maps ranking. Once verified, Google gives you a shareable review link; send it to a few recent brides directly rather than posting it publicly and hoping.

  7. Post an update once or twice a month

    Business Profile has a "Posts" feature, like a mini social feed attached to your listing — a new collection, a real wedding feature, a seasonal color. Listings that post regularly are treated as more active and tend to outrank ones that go quiet.

PART 2

Earn backlinks

A backlink is another website linking to yours. Google reads them as a vote of confidence — the more relevant, real sites that link to you, the more it trusts yours. There's no code fix for this; it's outreach and listings, done a little at a time.

Skip anything that promises "100 backlinks for $20." Paid link farms don't help and can actively get a site penalized. Every link below comes from a real, relevant listing or relationship — slower, but the kind that actually holds up.

Relationships (slower, but the strongest links)

  1. Ask photographers you've worked with for a feature

    Real weddings you've already supplied pieces for are the easiest pitch you have — you're not asking for a favor, you're offering them content. Wedding photographers regularly blog real weddings and credit every vendor with a link.

  2. Pitch a real-wedding feature to a bridal blog

    Sites like Style Me Pretty, Junebug Weddings, or regional Virginia wedding blogs accept real-wedding submissions from vendors, usually via the photographer as lead submitter. Every feature includes a vendor credit list with links.

  3. Cross-link with venues and other vendors

    If a venue or planner you've worked with has a "preferred vendors" page, ask to be added. Offer the same back if you ever build one on your site — a genuine two-way relationship, not a link swap for its own sake.

  4. Keep your Etsy shop's "About" section linking back

    You already have Etsy traffic — make sure your Etsy shop bio and any social profiles (Instagram, Pinterest, Facebook) link to ivoryandbloom.shop. Not a high-authority backlink, but it compounds with everything else.

A note on keyword research

I don't have access to real search-volume tools (Google Keyword Planner, Ahrefs, SEMrush) — the long-tail phrases already worked into your site (things like "custom color bridesmaid pouch" or "handmade flower crown wedding") are reasoned from what brides in this category plausibly search, not pulled from verified volume data.
