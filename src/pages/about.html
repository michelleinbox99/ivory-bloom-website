Where It Began

It Started With a Single Clay Flower

Before Ivory & Bloom, I spent decades as a professional designer — graphic design, web design, the discipline of making things beautiful and getting them right. Thirteen years ago I fell for something slower: shaping flowers by hand from clay, petal by petal.

What began as a small handmade clay-flower shop at Potomac Mills Mall became a quiet obsession — blooms that would never wilt, made one at a time. In time, customers began asking for pieces of their own, and the custom orders moved online.

Made in Her Colors
How Bridal Found Me

Then a Bride Asked for Something of Her Own

Could I make it in her colors? For her bridesmaids? For the littlest girl in the party? Those questions gave the work its purpose. Over the years I've created bridal accessories for many, many couples — and found my specialty in the pieces that carry a whole wedding party.

The bride's pouch and crown, coordinated bridesmaid gifts, ring bearer keepsakes, and flower girl sets sized for small hands — all designed to belong to the same story, without ever looking identical.

Decades of Design, Applied to One Wedding at a Time

A background in graphic and web design isn't a footnote here — it's how every commission is composed. Palette, proportion, the way a color photographs in golden light versus candlelight: these are professional instincts, brought to bear on your day.

Made to Be Kept
Why It Matters to Me

A Mother of Four, Making Things Meant to Be Kept

I'm a mother of four, so I know exactly how fast a day you planned for a year can pass — and how much a small, held thing can hold. That's what I make: not accessories, but keepsakes.

The pouch that hangs on the reception chair and then lives in a drawer for twenty years. The crown a flower girl asks to wear again. I love helping couples celebrate their day, and I love that a piece of it stays. And because you're working with the person who made it — not a returns department — there's always a real human to write to.

— Michelle, Founder
