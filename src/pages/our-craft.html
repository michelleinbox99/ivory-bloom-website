The Process

Four Steps, No Shortcuts

The making of a signature pouch, from a single square of satin to a hand-strung pearl handle.

01

The Cut

Satin is unforgiving — it shows every crease and shifts under the scissors if you rush it. Each piece is cut on the grain from a single length, so the sheen runs in one continuous direction and catches light in smooth bands.

02

The Gather

The signature ruffle isn't sewn on; it is the bag. A drawstring channel is stitched around the full circumference, then gathered by hand until the folds sit evenly — the single fussiest step, and the one no machine does well.

03

The Handle

Each handle is strung pearl by pearl on doubled, waxed cord, knotted internally so the pearls hold even if a strand were ever cut. Jewelry construction, applied to something a flower girl will absolutely swing in circles.

04

The Finish

Ribbon ties are heat-sealed and hand-tacked, seams enclosed so there's no raw edge anywhere your fingers can find. Every piece is photographed in natural light and checked against your order's color reference before it's wrapped.
