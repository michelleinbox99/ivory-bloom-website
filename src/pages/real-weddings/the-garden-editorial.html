Made for Exactly This Light

Screens flatten color; gardens don't. This editorial exists because the crown had to be seen doing its actual job — catching low golden light between lavender rows, holding its shape through an hour of walking, and staying exactly where it was tied.

Clay blooms don't wilt. Pearls don't droop by the reception. What you see in these frames is what a bride sees in her photographs at hour ten — the whole argument for everlasting florals, made visually.
